### STEP 1: THE "KILL ZONES" (AGGRESSIVE FILTERING)
Analyze the latest message. If it falls into ANY of these 4 Kill Zones, **IGNORE IT IMMEDIATELY**.

#### ZONE A: SOCIAL, RHETORICAL & META (The "Chatter" Zone)
*   **Tag Questions (Zero Tolerance)**: Seeking agreement/validation (e.g., "...right?", "...correct?", "...isn't it?", "对吧?", "是吗?", "对不对?", "行不行?").
*   **Personal Preferences**: Subjective likes/dislikes (e.g., "Which model do you like?", "Favorite editor?", "Do you prefer X?", "Coffee?").
*   **AI Identity**: Talking to the bot (e.g., "Who are you?", "Are you an AI?", "System prompts?").
*   **Vague / Rhetorical**: Short fragments or self-answered questions (e.g., "What next?", "Why? Because...", "Where is this going?").
*   **Clarification**: Confirming understanding (e.g., "What do you mean?", "Is this for Q3?", "这边说的是...?").

#### ZONE B: ROUTINE OPERATIONS & LOGISTICS (The "Work" Zone)
*   **Project Status**: Timelines, deadlines, owners, budget (e.g., "When can we ship?", "Is it done?", "Who owns this?", "Do we have budget?").
*   **IT & Setup**: VPN, Wi-Fi, Accounts, Installation, Access (e.g., "用公司VPN吗?", "How to connect?", "Where to download?", "Can I get access?").
*   **Recruitment & HR**: Interview logistics, stability, promotion, salary (e.g., "能学到东西吗?", "Stable?", "Remote?", "年后入职?", "Career growth?").
*   **Team Process**: Internal tools, Jira, release frequency (e.g., "Do you use Jira?", "Frequency of release?", "Can I write scripts?").

#### ZONE C: TECH SPECS, DEBUGGING & UI (The "Detail" Zone)
*   **Debugging / Troubleshooting**: Why it fails, error codes, connection issues (e.g., "为什么启动失败?", "连不上网?", "Why is X failing?", "Weak network error?").
*   **Version / Environment**: Branch, Prod/Staging, Version number (e.g., "你这是哪个版本?", "Is this prod?", "Which branch?", "Latest ver?").
*   **Technical Specs / Limits**: Capacity, Size, Sensor list, Platform, Tool choice (e.g., "最大支持多大?", "Capacity?", "Data density?", "Node or Python?", "What parser?", "需要开哪些传感器?").
*   **UI/UX Behavior**: Button clicks, Pop-ups, Flow logic (e.g., "Confirm exit?", "Does it pop up?", "What if I click X?", "这个垃圾桶删什么?").
*   **Hypothetical Edge Cases**: "What if user does X?" (Low-stakes operational assumptions).

#### ZONE D: KNOWLEDGE, METHODOLOGY & RESUME (The "Interview" Zone)
*   **Resume / Experience Checks**: Past projects, skills, "Have you done X?" (e.g., "你有没有弄过?", "Have you worked with X?", "What were you building?", "你测过吗?", "你跑过哪些自动化?").
*   **Standard Methodology**: Textbook "How-to" for testing/engineering (e.g., "弱网怎么测?", "兼容性怎么测?", "How to test X?", "Design patterns?", "安卓有天然方法吗?", "从哪些方向拆解?").
*   **Definitions**: "What is X?" (Even for AGI/Web3) (e.g., "Definition of AGI?", "What is Yotta Scale?", "What does that mean?").
*   **Subjective Reflection**: "What was hard?", "Thoughts?" (e.g., "有什么难做的地方?", "What was the hardest part?", "Give me your thoughts").
*   **Storytelling Requests**: "Tell me about...", "Explain the idea" (e.g., "讲讲这个思路", "Can you talk about it?").

#### ZONE E: SELF-DIRECTED QUESTIONS (ABSOLUTE IGNORE)
IGNORE any question where the speaker is asking themselves or their own team what to do, think, or plan (first-person forms such as "I", "we", "our"). Examples: "How do I...?", "How do we..?", "How can we...?", "What should we...?", "Should I/we...?", "Are we...?. These are internal planning or thinking-out-loud questions, NOT external strategic challenges.

### STEP 2: THE "SURVIVOR" TEST (EXTRACT ONLY IF...)
If a question survives the Kill Zones, apply this **Strategic Value Test**.
**Only extract if the answer is YES to ANY of the following:**

**1. Is it a Challenge?** (Core Competence / Logic / Integrity)
*   *Criterion*: Questions your ability, past failures, or logical consistency.
*   *Example*: "Why should we trust this architecture given the previous failure?"

**2. Is it an Accusation?** (Moral / Ethical / Institutional Attack)
*   *Criterion*: Direct attacks on character, intent, or impact on critical institutions. Even if not phrased as a question.
*   *Example*: "You are attacking a sacred institution on which every one of us depends." (Defend immediately).

**3. Is it a Deadlock?** (Strategic Pivot)
*   *Criterion*: Team is stuck on A vs B and needs a strategic breakthrough.
*   *Example*: "We are blocked on A vs B, what is the strategic pivot here?"

**4. Is it a Novel Crisis?** (High Stakes Problem Solving)
*   *Criterion*: Complex trade-off in a high-stakes scenario.
*   *Example*: "How do we solve the CAP theorem trade-off in this specific crisis?"

### STEP 4: STRICT DEDUPLICATION (SUBSTRINGS & OVERLAPS)
**Before finalizing the extraction/response, rigorously deduplicate questions that have a clear inclusion relationship:**
*   **No Substring/Inclusion Duplicates**: If one question is functionally a substring or a semantic subset of another (e.g., "Wasn't there supposed to be a problem with scaling..." vs "Wasn't there supposed to be a problem with scaling given that other companies..."), **ONLY KEEP THE FIRST APPEARING VERSION (the earliest one)**.
*   **Merge Context**: Do not extract the same core challenge multiple times simply because the speaker rephrased or expanded on it in subsequent sentences. Treat it as a single strategic crisis.
